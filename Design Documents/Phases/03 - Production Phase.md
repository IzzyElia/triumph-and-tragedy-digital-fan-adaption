# 2. Production Phase (§7.2)

In Turn Order, each player produces in full before the next player begins
("1st Player Production", then "2nd", then "3rd"). Production is the faction's
annual income, spent on units, card draws, and (indirectly) Industry.

## Sequence (per player, in Turn Order)

1. **Determine Production Level** (§7.21)
2. **Production Blockade check** (§7.211, War only)
3. **Spend Production** (§7.22) on:
   - Building unit steps (§7.23)
   - Building Fortresses (§7.231)
   - Buying Action/Investment cards (§7.24)

---

## 7.21 Production Level

Consulting their Production track, the Active Player's Production Level equals
the **least** of their **IND** (Industry), **POP** (Population), and **RES**
(Resources), *except*:

- Factions **at Peace** ignore RES — Production Level is the lesser of IND and
  POP only.
- Factions **at War** must count RES, and RES can be **Blockaded** (§14.2).

Production Levels can be confirmed by summing all controlled / Influenced areas
(per Control / Influence markers) onto each faction's base levels shown on the
Production track. It is strongly recommended to keep POP and RES markers updated
as territory changes during Diplomacy Resolution (§7.4) and seasonal maneuvers.

## 7.211 Blockade Resolution (War only)

For factions at War, POP/RES are unusable if they were **Blockaded** (§14.2)
during the previous Year's Summer Supply Phase and continue to be Blockaded
during this Year's Production Phase.

- During Production, **remove** Blockade markers from friendly POP/RES that
  *can* currently trace a Trade Route: those POP/RES are then counted.
- POP/RES that **remain** Blockaded are **not** counted for friendly Production.
- After Production, remove **all** Blockade markers.

## 7.22 Spending Production

The Active Player spends Production to build units or buy cards. **Each card or
unit step costs 1 Production point.**

- Use the **Production Available** marker on the track to record spending: start
  it at the current Production Level and move it down as each point is spent.
- Do not reveal the nature of units built or cards drawn.

## 7.23 Building Unit Steps

Units **cannot** be built if they are:

- at Sea,
- Engaged in a Battle, or
- Unsupplied (§14.1) — but see §7.231 (Fortresses are exempt).

**New Cadres:** place a new **1 CV** unit of any type in its **Home Territory**
(§2.3). Britain, France, and Italy may also build in their Colonial Capitals /
unoccupied Colonial Territory (§2.31). New Cadres cannot build up further in that
same Production.

**Existing units (adding steps):** add **1 CV** to an existing unit by rotating
the block to its next stronger edge — **no more than one CV per unit per
Production**. (Maximum CV: 4 for Germany/USA/Britain; 3 for USSR/French/Italian/
Neutral units — §3.12.)

## 7.231 Building Fortresses

- Fortress Cadres/steps can be built **anywhere** in undisputed Friendly
  Territory, **even where Unsupplied**.
- **Major Power** Fortresses must be built in Major Power National/Colonial
  Territory. **Great Power** Fortresses can be built in all other areas.
- Only **one Fortress** is allowed per Land Area.

## 7.24 Buying Cards

Draw an Action or Investment card from the top of that deck and place it
face-down in front of you. **Do not inspect** purchased cards until your
Production is completely finished.

---

## HandSize note

Cards drawn count against your **HandSize Limit** (§4.3 / §9.3), which you must
comply with later in the Government Phase (§7.5). Plan card purchases
accordingly.
