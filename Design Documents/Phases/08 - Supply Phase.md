# 7. Supply Phase (§14.0 / §10.3)

The final phase of **each Season**, following the completion of all Player Turns.
**Only factions at War** act here — factions at Peace ignore Supply entirely.

## Sequence

1. **Supply Check** — every faction at War checks all Ground units for a valid
   Supply Line (§14.1).
2. **(Summer only) Declare Blockades** of Enemy POP/RES sources (§14.22).

> Factions **at Peace** do **not** need to trace Supply Lines or check
> Blockades (§15.1). Supply is ignored during peacetime.

---

## 14.1 Supply Checks

During the Supply Phase, factions at War must check **all Ground units** for a
valid **Supply Line** (§14.11).

- Ground units **without** a Supply Line are **Unsupplied** and immediately take
  a forced step loss — *except* **Fortresses**, which ignore all Supply rules.
- **ANS units** are unaffected by Supply — *except* that forced Retreat applies
  to ANS units when all friendly Ground units are removed from a Disputed Area by
  Supply losses (§12.52).

## 14.11 Supply Lines

A **Supply Line** is a continuous chain of **Friendly** (Land/Sea) areas between
a Ground unit and a Supply Source — a controlled Factional Main-Capital or
SubCapital.

- Supply Lines may pass through **Friendly Territory** (including Disputed areas
  that are Friendly-controlled) and **Open Seas**.
- They **cannot** pass through Enemy-controlled areas, or Rival/Neutral Territory
  (**except Straits**, §1.3).

## 14.12 Production Supply Check

During Production, Unsupplied Ground units cannot build steps, nor can Ground
unit Cadres be built in Unsupplied locations (Fortresses are exempt). See also
the Production Phase file.

---

## 14.2 Blockade (Summer Supply Phase)

During the **Summer** Supply Phase, factions at War also **declare Blockades** of
Enemy POP/RES sources. Blockading denies an Enemy use of some of their controlled
POP/RES for Production.

### 14.21 Trade Routes
A **Trade Route** is a continuous chain of Land/Sea areas traced from a faction's
controlled POP/RES to its **MainCapital** (only!).

- Trade Routes can pass through **Friendly and Neutral** areas, including Open
  Seas. They **cannot** pass through Rival areas except for **Straits**, nor
  through any **Enemy-controlled** area or a Sea Area containing **Escaped Enemy
  Subs**.
- A Trade Route has **no more than one Sea Segment and one Land Segment**.

### 14.22 Blockades
During Summer Supply Phase, factions at War identify Enemy POP/RES currently
**unable to trace a valid Trade Route** and mark them (or their controlling
Capitals) with **Blockade markers**.

### 14.23 Confirming Blockades (during Production, §7.211)
At Production, **recheck** all POP/RES with Blockade markers for a valid Trade
Route. **Remove** the marker if a Trade Route can now be traced. POP/RES still
marked Blockaded do **not** count toward Production. **Remove all Blockade
markers after Production.**

### 14.24 Med Blockade
A Blockade of Trade Routes through the Mediterranean greatly reduces the value of
RES that must ship around Africa (POP is not affected). TransAfrica RES (red
triangles) can still trace around Africa; regular (black) RES cannot. Mark
affected RES with a **Med Blockade marker** (reverse of the Blockade marker).

> **Combination Blockades:** if an area is subject to a Med Blockade in Summer
> and a full Blockade at Production (or vice versa), only the **lesser** Med
> Blockade applies.

---

## Seasonal notes

- **Spring / Fall:** Supply Check only (no Blockade declaration).
- **Summer (§10.3):** Supply Check **and** Blockade declaration/Blockade Check.
- **Winter (§10.4):** a Winter Supply Phase follows the USSR's Winter turn and
  applies **only to units within the USSR**.

## DoW interaction (§15.3)

Factions **at War** must check friendly Ground units for valid Supply Lines
during **every** Supply Phase, must check for Blockades during **Summer**, and
during Production must count RES and check for Blockades.
