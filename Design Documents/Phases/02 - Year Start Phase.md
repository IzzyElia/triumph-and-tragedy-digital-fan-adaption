# 1. Year Start Phase (§7.1)

The first phase of every Game-Year. It advances the calendar, checks whether
anyone has already won, refreshes the card decks, hands out Peace Dividends, and
sets the Turn Order for the rest of the year.

## Sequence

1. **Advance Year** (§7.11)
2. **Victory Check / Reshuffle decks** (§7.12)
3. **Peace Dividends** (§7.13)
4. **Determine Turn Order** (§7.14)

---

## 7.11 Advance Year

Advance the **Year marker** one space on the Time track. (The very first
New Year of the standard game is 1936; the Short Game starts at 1939.)

## 7.12 Victory Check / Reshuffle

- **Check for Economic Victory** (§16.2): a faction that can demonstrate a total
  of **25 Victory Points** at the *start* of a New Year wins immediately. (See
  Victory, §16.0, for what counts toward VPs.)
- **Reshuffle** the Action and Investment decks, incorporating all discards back
  into their respective draw piles.

> Note: Military Victory (§16.3, controlling two Rival Main/SubCapitals at once)
> and Atomic Victory (§16.4) can be triggered *at any time*, not only here.

## 7.13 Peace Dividends

Deal **one Peace Dividend chit** (§4.4) face-down to each faction that is **at
Peace** (§15.1) — *unless* that faction Violated a Neutral (§15.4) or was
involved in a Battle during the previous Year.

- Peace Dividend chits have a secret Victory Point value of **0, 1, or 2**
  (§16.1). The owner keeps the value hidden.
- Chit distribution in the deck: 4× value-2, 12× value-1, 16× value-0.
- Peace Dividends reward (and create uncertainty around) peaceful development.

## 7.14 Determine Turn Order

Roll a die to determine this Year's **Turn Order**.

- Numbers in triangles at the ends of the Production tracks show the first
  player and the direction of rotation. For example, a roll of **2** yields the
  Turn Order **Axis → West → USSR**.
- The player currently taking their turn is the **Active Player**.
- The **Current Turn marker block** (orange) is passed around to the Active
  Player, upright face showing the direction of rotation.

Turn Order governs the **Production Phase** and **Government Phase** that follow,
and serves as the sequence for resolutions such as Diplomacy (§7.4) and Supply.

---

## Two-player / Short-game notes

- **Two-player game (§18.0):** Immediately after the Turn Order die roll, the
  Allied player declares which Ally (USSR or West) will be **Active** this
  Game-Year; the other Ally is **Passive** (§18.1) with constrained options.
- **Short Game (§17.0):** begins at 1939 with a special setup; each faction
  secretly draws **three** Peace Dividend chits during setup.
