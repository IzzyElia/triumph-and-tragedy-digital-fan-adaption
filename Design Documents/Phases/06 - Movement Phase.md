# 5. Movement Phase (§11.0)

The first phase of a **Player Turn** (Player Turns are taken in Command Order,
set in the Command Phase). The Active Player moves units; Combat (§12.0) follows
in the same Player Turn.

## Core rule

The Active Player may move a number of units **equal to the Command Value** of
the Command card they played this Season.

- Units move freely **through friendly units**, but all units **except Air
  Forces and Submarines must STOP** upon entering an **Enemy-occupied** area.
  (AFs and Subs may move *through* Enemy-occupied areas.)
- A unit can move by **Land Movement**, **Sea Movement**, or **Air Movement**,
  but these **cannot be combined** in a single move.
- Units **cannot** enter **Neutral Territory** (§1.14) before declaring a
  **Violation of Neutrality** (§15.4).
- Units **cannot** enter **Rival-Owned/occupied Territory** without previously
  **Declaring War** (§15.2). DoWs must be made *before* moving any units that
  turn.

> **Area Control:** moving into an area during a Movement Phase does **not**
> alter its control during that Phase — but it does affect the **following
> Combat Phase**, which governs Retreat and ReBase options.

---

## 11.1 Land Movement
Start the Movement Phase in a Land Area (Straits count, §1.3) and move through
contiguous Land Areas up to the unit's Movement Speed. **Stop** on entering an
area containing Enemy units. Terrain borders have no effect on Land Movement
*except* for Ground units that are **Engaging/Disengaging** (§11.5).
Naval/Submarine units may use Land Movement to enter an adjacent Coastal Land
Area **along the same coastline**, then must stop.

## 11.2 Sea Movement
Begin in a Sea, Ocean, Coastal Land, or Straits area. Move through contiguous
Sea/Ocean/Straits/Coastal Land areas up to Movement Speed, but **stop** on
entering a Coastal Land Area that is not Friendly/Neutral, or on a non-Friendly
Straits. May move through Rival-occupied Sea Areas without conflict, but **stop**
(except Subs) on entering an area with Enemy units.

- **11.21 Ocean Movement:** Oceans are large Sea Areas — they cost **two**
  movement points to enter.
- **11.22 Convoys:** a Ground unit using Sea Movement immediately becomes a
  **Convoy**. Convoys move **two Sea Areas** (four with LST Tech) but can neither
  Engage nor Disengage at Sea. They can **Sea Invade** (§11.221) into Enemy Land
  Areas.
- **11.221 Sea Invasions:** occur when Convoys move from Sea onto Enemy-
  controlled Land. Only **one Ground unit per phase** may Invade across a Coastal
  border (two with LST Tech). Invading Ground units **take no Combat Action** that
  Combat Phase — place them face-down. Accompanying Air/Naval/Sub units fire
  normally.

## 11.3 Air Movement
Air Forces move through both Land and Sea Areas and **need not stop** when
entering Enemy-occupied areas. AFs beginning in a Sea/Ocean Area may *only* move
into undisputed Friendly Land Territory and may not use Strategic Movement.

## 11.4 Strategic Movement
Units move **double** their Movement Speed when moving **strictly within Friendly
Territory** (§1.14), including Open Seas, and do **not** Engage/Disengage.

- Strategic Movement **cannot** combine with normal Land/Sea Movement.
- Strategic **Sea** Movement must still **STOP** on entering a Land Area (except
  Straits).
- Strategic **Air** Movement must **start and end** in Friendly Territory and may
  not pass through Neutral/Rival/Enemy Territory.
- Strategic Movement is **not allowed *into* a Battle** (but ReBasing is movement
  *out* of one — §13.0).

## 11.5 Engaging & Disengaging
- **Engaging:** entering an area containing Enemy or Armed Minor units.
- **Disengaging (§11.51):** removing a unit from a Battle by movement or Retreat.
  Units can only Disengage into adjacent **Friendly** areas (including Open Seas)
  that are not Battles. *Exception:* Subs and Air Forces can Disengage into
  adjacent Disputed/Enemy-controlled areas, provided they move **through** and do
  not remain (which would be re-Engaging). Units unable to Disengage must remain
  in the Battle.

### Movement-phase combat concepts (apply now, resolve in Combat Phase)
- **11.52 Border Limits (Land):** terrain restricts how many **Ground** units can
  Engage/Disengage across a border in a single phase — **3** across Plains; **2**
  across River/Forest; **1** across Mountain/Coastal/Straits. (ANS units are
  exempt.)
- **11.53 BattleGroups (Sea):** ANS units that start together and move along the
  same route into the same Sea Battle form a BattleGroup; only one BattleGroup
  per side may join a Sea Battle per Combat Round (§12.21).
- **11.54 Aggression:** entering unoccupied Enemy Territory, or an area with only
  Enemy units, is **Aggression** — the entering player is the **Aggressor** in
  that Battle; the original occupant is the **Owner**.
- **11.55 Raids:** Aggression by ANS units without Ground Support (such ANS units
  are "Raiders") — Raids cannot conquer Territory or block Retreats.

---

Once movement is complete, proceed to the **Combat Phase** (`06 - Combat
Phase.md`) for this same Player Turn.
