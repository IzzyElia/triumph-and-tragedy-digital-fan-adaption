# Setup (§5.0) — Pre-Game

Setup is **not** a game phase, but it is the first thing that happens in a game,
before the 1936 New Year begins. It places each faction's starting units, sets
each faction's starting economic levels (POP / IND / RES), seeds starting hands,
and puts everyone "at Peace."

After setup is complete, play begins with the **1936 New Year** (§7.0) — see
`01 - Year Start Phase.md`.

---

## General setup procedure (§5.0)

1. **Deploy starting units.** Each faction deploys **1 CV Cadres** of any desired
   type into its National/Colonial Cities — **one Cadre per POP** (the number of
   circles on the City; e.g. London has POP 3, so three Cadres). The **Axis
   deploys double** this amount (e.g. six in Berlin). Britain and France also
   receive **specific additional units** (shown by icons on the map — fleets,
   fortresses, infantry). See the per-faction tables below.
   - Keep all **unused blocks hidden off-map**.
   - Because of Germany's renunciation of the Versailles Treaty (the rearmament
     race), **most units start as 1 CV Cadres** (§5.0 / "1936 Starting Forces").
2. **Set economic markers.** Place the **POP, RES, and IND marker blocks** at
   their initial levels on each faction's Production track (values below).
3. **Set Peace/War markers.** Place each faction's **"at Peace with"** markers in
   the DoW boxes — all factions begin **at Peace** (§15.1).
4. **Set RES blocks to Peace side.** Each RES marker block has both label types on
   opposite faces: **colored-triangle side up while at Peace** (RES ignored),
   black-triangle side up when at War (RES counts toward Production). Start them
   colored-triangle up.
5. **Place the Current Turn marker** (orange block) ready for Turn Order.
6. **Draw starting hands** of Action cards (the **Axis draws a double HandSize**).
7. **Begin the 1936 New Year** (§7.0), including the first Peace Dividends.

## Starting economic levels

| Faction | IND | POP | RES | Factory Cost | HandSize Limit |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|--------------|----------------|
| **Axis** (Germany + Italy) | 12 | 11 | 6 | 5 | 7 |
| **West** (Britain + France) | 7 | 12 | 11 | 6 | 8 |
| **USSR** | 9 | 12 | 11 | 7 | 6 |

> At Peace, RES is ignored for Production (§7.21), so the at-Peace starting
> Production Level is the lesser of **IND** and **POP**.
> (Factory Cost is the number of Factories needed to raise IND one level, §7.32.)

|----------|------|

| Other USSR Cities | 1 Cadre each (5 total, to reach 12 CV) |

- **USSR total: 10 units / 12 CV.**
- **Draw 6 Action cards.**
- IND 9 (Factory Cost 7) · POP 12 · RES 11.

> **Note on the Moscow stack:** the rulebook text (§5.3) phrases this as
> "3 Cadres in Moscow … Total: 12 units / 12 CV." Moscow instead begins with a
> **single 3 CV Infantry** (the USSR national CV maximum, §3.12) rather than
> three separate 1 CV cadres. This preserves the **12 CV** starting strength but
> means the USSR begins with **10 units** rather than 12.

## Short Game setup — 1939 (§17.0)

The Short Game starts at **1939** instead of 1936:

1. Place the same **Starting Forces (units/CV) and POP/RES as the 1936 setup**
   above.
2. Roll a special **Setup Player Order** die. Then, referencing the Short Game
   Table, each faction:
   - Adds the **additional (+) Cadres/CV** within its Home Territory,
   - Draws the listed **Action and Investment cards**,
   - Places its **IND marker** at the 1939 value,
   - Secretly draws **three Peace Dividend chits**.
3. The **Axis controls Austria**: place a Control marker and a **2 CV Infantry**
   there, and adjust **POP +1**.
4. The **Axis plays 7 Diplomacy cards unopposed**, marks the resulting
   Influence/Control, adjusts POP/RES on its track, then discards those cards.
5. **Begin 1939 with the New Year.**

**Short Game Setup table (1939):**

| | West | Axis | USSR |
|---|------|------|------|
| **+Cadres / CV** | +8 | +20 | +14 |
| **Action Cards** | 12 | 18 | 10 |
| **Investment Cards** | 4 | 4 | 4 |
| **1939 IND** | 10 | 15 | 12 |
| **Peace Dividends** | 3 | 3 | 3 |

## Two-player setup notes (§18.0)

One player runs both the **USSR and West** (the "Allies"); the other runs the
**Axis**.

- All Allied **USSR/West Cadres starting in Baku and India must be Fortresses**.
- Initial Setup cards are dealt **face down**. The **Passive Ally** (§18.2)
  cannot inspect or play **half** of these cards during the first Year (§18.22) —
  of its initial Setup cards, half (round down) go into its hand and the rest
  into the face-down **Passive Stack**.
